Such systems have the advantage of (i) having simple composition in comparison to active systems, which reduces costs, e of (ii) offering operational reliability, which results in safety. Passive Cooling Systems are employed, for example, in electronic components, engines and energy generation. In particular, most of new nuclear power plant designs is provided with at least one natural convection circuit for safety function, and there are nuclear reactor designs whose normal operation occurs by natural convection. However, Passive Cooling Systems are subject to thermos-hydraulic instabilities, which requires a precise physical characterization of the problem and fine adjustments of numerical parameters in the modeling process. {%- endmark %} {% mark link-%} - [read more][1] [1]: {{media_url('html/research/spr/text.html')}} - [publications][2] [2]: {{media_url('html/research/spr/publications.html')}} {%- endmark %}
