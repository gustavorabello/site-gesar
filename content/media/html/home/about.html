--- extends: post.j2 default_block: text title: GESAR - Group for Studies and Environmental Simulations in Water Reservoirs description: GESAR - About --- # Short History of GESAR   The GESAR group (Group for Studies and Environmental Simulations in Water Reservoirs) was founded in 2005 after a R&D project managed by the brazilian power [Furnas Centrais Elétricas S.A.][1] an requested to State University of Rio de Janeiro for the deployment of a simulator of the dynamics of hydropower plant reservoirs. Designed to have commercial quality, the software *FERSim* - as it was known - was intended to study the dynamics of biomass incorporation during the step of filling of the reservoirs.   GESAR group is part of the [Faculty of Engineering][3] at the [State University of Rio de Janeiro][2], which is located at Campus Fonseca Telles, at the heart of the park Quinta da Boa Vista, in the neighbourhood of São Cristóvão, metropolitan region of Rio. The pioneer investments at GESAR overpassed US$1 mi, thereby affording it to carry out the required limnological studies for water bodies monitoring as well as three-dimensional computational simulations of high complexity related to their dynamic motion. GESAR's laboratories are specialized in both experimental and computational threads that hold from external field survey to chemical analyses at microscopic level. Currently, the laboratories are being redesigned. [1]: http:://www.furnas.com.br [2]: http://www.uerj.br [3]: http://www.eng.uerj.br   Concerning the scientific collaboration, GESAR's partnerships are spreaded worlwide with: [CWR - Center for Water Research][11] (Austrália), [UNA - Universidad Nacional de Asunción][12] (Paraguai) -, [TU Delft - Universidade Tecnológica de Delft][13] (Holanda), [LTCM/EPFL - Laboratoire de Transfert de Chaleur et de Masse][14] (Suíça) e [ULB - Free University of Brussels][15] (Bélgica), [Université du Havre][16] (France), [University of Edinburgh][17] (UK, Scotland), [University of Glasglow][18] (UK, Scotland) and [University of Strathclyde][19] (UK, Scotland), among local institutions. [11]: http://www.cwr.uwa.edu.au/homepage.php [12]: http://www.una.py [13]: http://www.tudelft.nl/en [14]: http://ltcm.epfl.ch [15]: http://www.ulb.ac.be//sciences/nlpc [16]: https://www.univ-lehavre.fr [17]: http://www.ed.ac.uk [18]: http://www.gla.ac.uk [19]: https://www.strath.ac.uk   Besides carrying on with studies in the fields of analytical chemistry and data processing, GESAR is keen to theoretical and applied research in biology, computational sciences and mechanics, although upholds a traditional root in computer programming and finite element methods. Such inherent features led GESAR to deploy a strong background in the craft of the computational modelling of transport phenomena. Through its projects, GESAR's portfolio comprises 26 projects whose total amount of investments granted by the main Brazilian fostering agencies (CAPES, CNPq, FINEP, FAPERJ) sum about US$ 3 mi.   Being a Technological Development Unit, the GESAR group is able to provide consulting as well as research services to public and private companies. GESAR was also a cornerstone to start up the UERJ's Graduate Program in Mechanical Engineering (PPG-EM) activities in 2005. Initially, PPG-EM could offer academic masters courses only. However, since the establishment of the doctoral program in 2010, PPG-EM's numbers have been increasing toward forming new science personnel. Hitherto (final of 2014), 15 masters and 3 doctor degrees has been conferred.     # Campus Fonseca Telles' Neighbourhood   Campus Fonseca Telles of State University of Rio de Janeiro is located at São Cristóvão, near to a few places of cultural and touristic attractions, such as:   - [The Rio's Zoo][30]; - [The National Museum of Natural History (curated by the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro][31]; - [The São Cristóvão's Traditional Fair][32]; - [Quinta da Boa Vista Municipal Park][33]; [30]: http://www.riodejaneiroaqui.com/portugues/q_riozoo.html [31]: http://www.museunacional.ufrj.br [32]: http://www.feiradesaocristovao.org.br/ [33]: http://www.riodejaneiroaqui.com/portugues/quinta.html   Some pictures of the campus' neighbourhood are displayed below:
