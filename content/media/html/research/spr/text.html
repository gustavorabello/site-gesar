Passive Cooling Systems

EXPERIMENTAL PROJECT ON NATURAL CONVECTION LOOPS

RESEARCH TEAM
  • Danillo Soares
  • Gabriel Cerqueira
  • João Pedro Bastos
  • Leon Lima

    OBJECTIVE
    Experimental and Computational study of single-phase Natural Circulation Loops

    CONTEXT
    A large number of papers published in the last ten years with numerical and experimental results from natural circulation loops shows the importance of this topic. With respect to applications in nuclear industry, the circuit which cools the nuclear reactors in Brazilian power plants -- Angra 1, 2 and 3 -- may operate by natural convection in case of unavailability of the reactor coolant pumps, and the dynamic behavior of such flow must be well understood at the design phase. Another application is the facility for spent fuel storage, under design by Eletronuclear, which will count with a fully passive heat removal system.

    RESEARCH FOCUS
    • similarity parameters
    • influence of system's parameters -- diameter, heat load, cooler heat transfer coefficient etc. -- on thermo-hydraulic stability.
    • effect of water mass intermediating the loop's heater and the primary heat load, e.g., integration between circuit and pool, on the system's dynamic behavior
    • computational modeling: friction correlation and numerical formulation.
    • 3D simulation
    • stability analysis
    MOTIVATION
    At the same time that applications of single-phase Passive Cooling Systems grow, notably in safety systems in nuclear installations, there isn't so far a well accepted friction correlation for natural convection flows. In deed, correlations for forced convection are normally used in natural convection circuits. Additionally, there isn't available results taking integration between loop and pool into account.

    FINAL PRODUCT
    • establishment of a criterion for selection of most appropriate friction factor for natural circulation loops or a proposition of a new correlation.
    • computational code (computer program, free for download) for sizing and transient simulation of Passive Cooling Systems.
    • to provide experimental data for single-phase Passive Cooling Systems.
    PARTIAL RESULTS

    A 1D model has been implemented in Matlab® to simulate a natural circulation loop with heater and cooler as piping sections. The model consists of conservation equations for momentum and energy, both for mean quantities in the tube cross section. Momentum equation is also integrated along the loop's length. The following processes are neglected in the model:
    • heat conduction
    • heat generation by friction
    • heat transfer across pipe walls (except in heater and cooler).
    The governing equations are:

    Momentum
    $$\frac{L}{A}\frac{dw}{dt} = -\left(f\frac{L}{D}+K\right)\frac{w^2}{2\rho_0A^2} + \rho_0g\beta\oint Tdz$$



    Energy

    pipes:

    $$\frac{\partial T}{\partial t}+\frac{w}{\rho_0A}\frac{\partial T}{\partial s} = 0$$




    heater:

    $$\frac{\partial T}{\partial t}+\frac{w}{\rho_0A}\frac{\partial T}{\partial s} = \frac{Q_{N}}{L_hA\rho_0c_p}$$




    cooler:

    $$\frac{\partial T}{\partial t}+\frac{w}{\rho_0A}\frac{\partial T}{\partial s} = -\frac{4U_c(T-T_s)}{D\rho_0c_p}$$

    EXPERIMENT

    The experimental setup consists on a rectangular loop, 1 m tall and 0.8 m wide, with horizontal heater and cooler. The thermal load is supplied to the system by means of three electrical resistances 667 W each, building up a total power capacity of 2000 W. The cooler is a tube in tube heat exchanger, where the work fluid of the loop flows through the inner tube and a cooling fluid flows (forced) through the outer tube (annulus). The system is equipped with 4 digital temperature sensors, positioned at the inlet and outlet of heater and cooler. Insturmentation is performed via a Arduino MEGA 2560 board.
