Welcome to GESAR!

 

The GESAR group (Group for Studies and Environmental Simulations in Water Reservoirs) was founded in 2005 after a R&D project managed by the brazilian power utility company Furnas Centrais Elétricas S.A. an requested to State University of Rio de Janeiro for the deployment of a simulator of the dynamics of hydropower plant reservoirs. Committed to developing applied research, GESAR's portfolio comprises 26 projects that encompass different fields of study, such as numerical simulations, water quality assessment and database development. The total amount of investment in these projects are granted by the main Brazilian fostering agencies (CAPES, CNPq, FINEP, FAPERJ) and sum about US$ 3 mi. GESAR's partnerships are spreaded worlwide: CWR - Center for Water Research (Australia); UNA - Universidad Nacional de Asunción (Paraguay); ULB - Free University of Brussels (Belgium); Ecole Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (Switzerland), and other local institutions. Currently, the GESAR group is a Technological Development Unit at State University of Rio de Janeiro able to provide consulting as well as research services to public and private companies. GESAR was also the pioneer to start up the UERJ's Graduate Program in Mechanical Engineering activities in 2005, which, focused on forming new science personnel, has conferred hitherto 15 masters and 3 doctor degrees.
